*EEOC SUES TOTAL SYSTEMS SERVICES FOR DISABILITY DISCRIMINATION AND RETAL=
IATION
*
*"Global Payments Company Denied Worker=92s Pleas for Remote Work Due to =
High Risk for COVID-19 Infection, Federal Agency Charges"*

ATLANTA =96 Total Systems Services, LLC, a global payments processing com=
pany based in Columbus, Georgia, violated federal law by denying repeated=
 requests by an employee with a disability for remote work as a reasonabl=
e accommodation due to increased risks related to COVID-19, the U.S. Equa=
l Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) charged in a lawsuit filed tod=
ay. The company further violated the law by retaliating against her for t=
aking medical leave to avoid exposure, the EEOC said.

=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 According to the EEOC=92s suit, from May through Augu=
st 2020, a customer service representative with a disability in a Total S=
ystems=92 call center repeatedly requested to work remotely as a reasonab=
le accommodation because of her high-risk status with respect to COVID-19=
. At the time, the employee=92s call center co-workers were regularly tes=
ting positive for the virus, the EEOC said.

=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 On the advice of her doctor, the employee requested r=
emote work after a May 2020 workplace COVID-19 exposure. Total Systems de=
nied the employee=92s reasonable accommodation request.

=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 To avoid further exposure =97 and the increased risks=
 she faced if she contracted COVID-19 =97 the employee went on medical le=
ave until a remote position became available. By the time the employee=92=
s leave expired in July 2020, most of the department was working remotely=
, but Total Systems continued to deny the employee=92s reasonable accommo=
dation request. The employee was forced to resign in August 2020 to ensur=
e her safety, the EEOC said.

=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 Total Systems=92 conduct violates the Americans with =
Disabilities Act (ADA). The EEOC filed suit ("Civil Action No. 1:23-CV-13=
11-WMR-JSA") in U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Georgia,=
 Atlanta Division, after first attempting to reach a pre-litigation settl=
ement via its conciliation process. The EEOC is seeking back pay, compens=
atory damages, and punitive damages for the employee, as well as injuncti=
ve relief to prevent future discrimination.

=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =93The ADA requires employers to reasonably accommoda=
te employees with disabilities =96 and this is not a suggestion,=94 said =
Marcus G. Keegan, regional attorney for the EEOC=92s Atlanta District Off=
ice. Keegan said. =93Employers who retaliate against employees for exerci=
sing their rights under the ADA, as Total Systems did here, expose themse=
lves to additional liability under the statute.=94

=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 Darrell Graham, district director of the Atlanta offi=
ce, said, =93The employee did everything she could to protect her health =
and perform her job at the same time. The EEOC is resolute in its mission=
 to enforce the ADA and protect the rights of employees with disabilities=
.=94

<p align=3D"center"><strong><font size=3D"5">EEOC SUES TOTAL SYSTEMS SERV=
ICES FOR DISABILITY DISCRIMINATION AND RETALIATION<br></font></strong><br=
><strong><em><font size=3D"4">Global Payments Company Denied Worker=92s P=
leas for Remote Work Due to High Risk for COVID-19 Infection, Federal Age=
ncy Charges</font></em></strong></p>
<p>ATLANTA =96 Total Systems Services, LLC, a global payments processing =
company based in Columbus, Georgia, violated federal law by denying repea=
ted requests by an employee with a disability for remote work as a reason=
able accommodation due to increased risks related to COVID-19, the U.S. E=
qual Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) charged in a lawsuit filed =
today. The company further violated the law by retaliating against her fo=
r taking medical leave to avoid exposure, the EEOC said.</p>
<p>=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 According to the EEOC=92s suit, from May through A=
ugust 2020, a customer service representative with a disability in a Tota=
l Systems=92 call center repeatedly requested to work remotely as a reaso=
nable accommodation because of her high-risk status with respect to COVID=
-19. At the time, the employee=92s call center co-workers were regularly =
testing positive for the virus, the EEOC said.</p>
<p>=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 On the advice of her doctor, the employee requeste=
d remote work after a May 2020 workplace COVID-19 exposure. Total Systems=
 denied the employee=92s reasonable accommodation request.</p>
<p>=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 To avoid further exposure =97 and the increased ri=
sks she faced if she contracted COVID-19 =97 the employee went on medical=
 leave until a remote position became available. By the time the employee=
=92s leave expired in July 2020, most of the department was working remot=
ely, but Total Systems continued to deny the employee=92s reasonable acco=
mmodation request. The employee was forced to resign in August 2020 to en=
sure her safety, the EEOC said.</p>
<p>=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 Total Systems=92 conduct violates the Americans wi=
th Disabilities Act (ADA). The EEOC filed suit (<em>Civil Action No. 1:23=
-CV-1311-WMR-JSA</em>) in U.S. District Court for the Northern District o=
f Georgia, Atlanta Division, after first attempting to reach a pre-litiga=
tion settlement via its conciliation process. The EEOC is seeking back pa=
y, compensatory damages, and punitive damages for the employee, as well a=
s injunctive relief to prevent future discrimination.</p>
<p>=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =93The ADA requires employers to reasonably accomm=
odate employees with disabilities =96 and this is not a suggestion,=94 sa=
id Marcus G. Keegan, regional attorney for the EEOC=92s Atlanta District =
Office. Keegan said. =93Employers who retaliate against employees for exe=
rcising their rights under the ADA, as Total Systems did here, expose the=
mselves to additional liability under the statute.=94</p>
<p>=A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 =A0 Darrell Graham, district director of the Atlanta o=
ffice, said, =93The employee did everything she could to protect her heal=
th and perform her job at the same time. The EEOC is resolute in its miss=
ion to enforce the ADA and protect the rights of employees with disabilit=
ies.=94</p>
