*PARRIS PIZZA COMPANY TO PAY $150,000=A0**TO SETTLE EEOC RACE HARASSMENT =

*"Domino=92s Pizza Franchisee Subjected Black Employees to Racial Slurs a=
nd Other Race-Based Harassment, Federal Agency Charged"*

BUFFALO, N.Y. =96 Parris Pizza Company, LLC, the former owner of a Domino=
=92s Pizza franchise in Olean, New York, has agreed to pay $150,000 and p=
rovide other relief to resolve a racial harassment lawsuit filed by the U=
.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), the federal agency an=
nounced today.

According to the EEOC=92s lawsuit, beginning in at least 2019, Parris Piz=
za permitted Black employees to be harassed by White coworkers.=A0 The mi=
streatment included two shift managers=92 regular and open use of slurs d=
uring every shift.=A0 Among other incidents, one of the managers mimicked=
 the voice of a slave owner and called an employee =93boy=94 while the ot=
her manager stood by and laughed.=A0 Black employees repeatedly complaine=
d about the harassment, but Parris Pizza did not take any action.=A0 Beca=
use of the harassment and Parris Pizza=92s failure to do anything about i=
t, one employee was compelled to resign.

Such alleged conduct violates Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, =
which prohibits employers from discriminating and harassing employees on =
the basis of race.=A0 The EEOC filed suit in U.S. District Court for the =
Western District of New York ("EEOC v. Parris Pizza Co., LLC f/d/b/a Domi=
no=92s Pizza", Civil Action No. 1:23-cv-000111-JLS-LGF) after first attem=
pting to reach a pre-litigation settlement through its conciliation proce=
ss.

Under the five-year consent decree settling the suit, Parris Pizza will p=
ay $150,000 to the employees who were harassed and will send an apology l=
etter to all former employees.=A0 Parris Pizza no longer operates any bus=
inesses, however, the consent decree provides that if Parris Pizza opens =
any new businesses during the duration of the decree, it will institute r=
obust anti-discrimination policies and provide training on the requiremen=
ts of federal anti-discrimination laws for all employees.=A0

=93The law requires employers to thoroughly investigate complaints of rac=
ial harassment and take effective action to end a hostile work environmen=
t,=94 said Jeffrey Burstein, regional attorney for the EEOC=92s New York =
District Office.=A0 =93We are pleased Parris Pizza has agreed to provide =
relief to the Black employees who had to go to work every day and endure =
incessant use of racial slurs.=94

=93This consent decree sends a message to employers that they cannot turn=
 a blind eye to racial harassment,=94 said EEOC=92s New York Acting Distr=
ict Director Timothy Riera.=A0 =93The EEOC=92s Buffalo Local Office will =
continue to be vigilant in its efforts to identify employers who fail to =
appropriately address racial harassment to ensure that such unlawful cond=
uct stops.=94

<p style=3D"text-align: center; -webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-si=

 margin: 0 0 15px;" align=3D"center"><strong>PARRIS PIZZA COMPANY TO PAY $1=
50,000&nbsp;</strong><strong>TO SETTLE EEOC RACE HARASSMENT LAWSUIT</strong=
></p>
<p style=3D"text-align: center; -webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-si=
ze-adjust: 100%; line-height: 1.4; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 15px;=
 margin: 0 0 15px;" align=3D"center"><strong><em>Domino=92s Pizza Franchise=
e Subjected Black Employees to Racial Slurs and Other Race-Based Harassment=
, Federal Agency Charged</em></strong></p>
<p style=3D"-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-size-adjust: 100%; lin=
e-height: 1.4; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 15px; margin: 0 0 15px;">=
BUFFALO, N.Y. =96 Parris Pizza Company, LLC, the former owner of a Domino=
=92s Pizza franchise in Olean, New York, has agreed to pay $150,000 and pro=
vide other relief to resolve a racial harassment lawsuit filed by the U.S. =
Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), the federal agency announce=
d today.</p>
<p style=3D"-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-size-adjust: 100%; lin=
e-height: 1.4; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 15px; margin: 0 0 15px;">=
According to the EEOC=92s lawsuit, beginning in at least 2019, Parris Pizza=
 permitted Black employees to be harassed by White coworkers.&nbsp; The mis=
treatment included two shift managers=92 regular and open use of slurs duri=
ng every shift.&nbsp; Among other incidents, one of the managers mimicked t=
he voice of a slave owner and called an employee =93boy=94 while the other =
manager stood by and laughed.&nbsp; Black employees repeatedly complained a=
bout the harassment, but Parris Pizza did not take any action.&nbsp; Becaus=
e of the harassment and Parris Pizza=92s failure to do anything about it, o=
ne employee was compelled to resign.</p>
<p style=3D"-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-size-adjust: 100%; lin=
e-height: 1.4; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 15px; margin: 0 0 15px;">=
Such alleged conduct violates Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, wh=
ich prohibits employers from discriminating and harassing employees on the =
basis of race.&nbsp; The EEOC filed suit in U.S. District Court for the Wes=
tern District of New York (<em>EEOC v. Parris Pizza Co., LLC f/d/b/a Domino=
=92s Pizza</em>, Civil Action No. 1:23-cv-000111-JLS-LGF) after first attem=
pting to reach a pre-litigation settlement through its conciliation process=
.</p>
<p style=3D"-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-size-adjust: 100%; lin=
e-height: 1.4; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 15px; margin: 0 0 15px;">=
Under the five-year consent decree settling the suit, Parris Pizza will pay=
 $150,000 to the employees who were harassed and will send an apology lette=
r to all former employees.&nbsp; Parris Pizza no longer operates any busine=
sses, however, the consent decree provides that if Parris Pizza opens any n=
ew businesses during the duration of the decree, it will institute robust a=
nti-discrimination policies and provide training on the requirements of fed=
eral anti-discrimination laws for all employees.&nbsp;</p>
<p style=3D"-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; -ms-text-size-adjust: 100%; lin=
e-height: 1.4; font-family: helvetica; font-size: 15px; margin: 0 0 15px;">=
=93The law requires employers to thoroughly investigate complaints of racia=
l harassment and take effective action to end a hostile work environment,=
=94 said Jeffrey Burstein, regional attorney for the EEOC=92s New York Dist=
rict Office.&nbsp; =93We are pleased Parris Pizza has agreed to provide rel=
