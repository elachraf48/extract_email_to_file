added value to one another. Humanity's ascent to the world's apex

Building the consumer internet on advertising was the original sin.
Serving ads next to our search bars and on our homepages gave us a free
internet via layaway, but the payments have become untenable.

Separating the consumer and the customer creates an alignment problem.
We need to reconnect them. Paid content is to an ad-supported model what
renewables are to internal combustion. Subscriptions connect end users
to content providers directly, in that they are customers, vs. products
being harvested for the ad model's true customer, the advertiser.
It's a basic observation, but still holds that Fox and Meta have
levied a great deal more damage than Netflix or LinkedIn (most of its
revenue comes from recruitment services), neither of which is depressing
our teens or being weaponized by the GRU.

Subscriptions are not the only payment model - micropayments are the
most rational way to connect value with price. However, micropayments
continue to be the missing piece, the fission of media: promise vs.
practical performance. One of the downsides of moving away from free
media is that we'll further bifurcate into a world of Android (ad
supported) vs. iOS (premium). I've always felt the assumption that
"people can't afford" subscription content was a bit hyperbolic, as

<h4>Taking the Temperature Down</h4>
<p>Climate scientists have proven rising temperatures are causing widesprea=

<p>Building the consumer internet on advertising was the original sin. Serv=

t via layaway, but the payments have become untenable.</p>=0A
<p>Separating the consumer and the customer creates an alignment problem. W=

<p>Subscriptions are not the only payment model =E2=80=94 micropayments are=

 we=E2=80=99ll further bifurcate into a world of Android (ad supported) vs.=

=E2=80=99t afford" subscription content was a bit hyperbolic, as almost two=
